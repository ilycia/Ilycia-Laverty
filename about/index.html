About

My design practice is born from a deep respect for land, place and intuitive process.

Across architecture, interiors and objects, I create spaces that resonate with the people who inhabit them and the environments they belong to. My work is guided by a heart-brain approach to design, balancing analytical thinking with intuition, observation and lived experience.

I am particularly interested in regenerative architecture and the relationship between people and living systems. Through climate responsiveness, ecological thinking and careful material selection, I seek to create spaces that contribute positively to both human wellbeing and the environments they occupy.

Alongside measurable systems, I explore the subtler qualities of experience — harmony, proportion, atmosphere and the ways space can influence how we feel, connect and remember. The result is architecture that aims not only to function well, but to feel alive.

Whether designing a home that gives back more than it takes, a room that invites stillness, or an object that carries story, my work is driven by a desire to bring beauty, coherence and life to the built world.

Based in Aotearoa New Zealand and informed by my diverse whakapapa, I work with natural materials, intuitive form-making and deep site connection.

I am an architectural designer, artist and researcher currently exploring regenerative architecture, living systems and Bio Frequency Design.
